FATAL SHOTS

In 2019, 998 people were killed by US Police.
This narrative takes you through the numbers for years 2015-2020 and their relation to certain attributes.

STEADY NUMBER

Over the past few years, number of people killed by police has stayed steady.

The average number of fatal shooting stands at 984 between 2015 and 2019. This chart shows the number of fatalities for years from 2015 to 2019 and their numbers for each month.
For 2020, the number of fatalities as of May stands at 365 cases. This number is lower compared to May number from previous 5 years, however, an upward trend is visible.

NUMBERS BY STATE

California, Texas and Florida have the highest number of deaths.

California, Texas and Florida have been consistently ranked as states with highest number of deaths. Arizona, Colorado, Georia and Washington state are trailing the top 3. These states also have some of the highest population in the country.

RACE FACTOR

Compared to other races, Blacks and Hispanics are killed at higher rates. This visualization uses 2019 population and fatality count.

White Americans form the majority of people killed at just over 48% compared to 29% and 19% of Blacks and Hispanics respectively. However, there are 4 times more White americans than Black americans. Black and Hispanics are 2 to 3 times more likely to be shot and killed.

AGE FACTOR

A large marjority of those killed are between 21 and 40 years old. This visualization uses data from 2019.

Most people killed are between 21 and 60 years old with 21-40 year olds forming the majority across differenct races. In 2019, the youngest killed was 14 years while the oldest was 80 years old.
