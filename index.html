FATAL SHOTS

By US Law Enforcement

In 2019, 998 people were killed.

STEADY NUMBER

Over the past few years, number of people killed by police has stayed steady.

The average number fatal shooting stands at 984 between 2015 and 2019. This chart shows the number of fatalities for years from 2015 to 2019 and their numbers for each month.

NUMBERS BY STATE

California, Texas, Florida and Arizona are states that have the highest number of deaths from US Law enforcement.

California, Texas and Florida have been consistently ranked as states with highest number of deaths. These states also have some of the highest population in the country.

RACE FACTOR

Compared to other races, Blacks and Hispanics are killed at higher rates.

White Americans form the majority of people killed at just over 48% compared to 29% and 19% of Blacks and Hispanics respectively. However, there are 4 times more White americans than Black americans. Black and Hispanics are 2 to 3 times more likely to be shot and killed. These disparities have led to renewed discussions about Race as a factor in these fatal shootings recent years.
